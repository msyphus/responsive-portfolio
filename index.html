About Me

I'm a second-generation programmer. My dad introduced me to programming when I was a child. Showing my friends that I could draw silly pictures and add music to them with code always made me feel smart. Some of my friends were impressed, so I started teaching them, too. Fast forward and one of those friends was so interested in programming that he got a degree in software engineering and became a programmer. Although I enjoyed coding I, chose to become a scientist.

I had a successful career in the aquaculture feed industry formulating diets for all kinds of aquatic animals (and even some dogs and reptiles), overseeing quality assurance and quality control, and leading R&D projects. My work frequently led to using coding (R for statistics, VBA for work documents) or working with the IT Department to improve the user experience of the software applications we were using (ERP, MES, CRM, Business Intelligence, databases, and formulation software). Eventually, I realized that my favorite days at work were the days when I was coding or working with improving software solutions. Therefore, I'm making the switch to become a full stack developer.
